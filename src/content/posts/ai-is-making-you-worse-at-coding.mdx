# {frontmatter.title}

A lot of people think artifical intelligence is the answer to all of our problems. I've also heard if you aren't using artificial intelligence in your workflow, then you'll soon be left behind.

I think that's incredibly naive.

Artificial intelligence and "productivity" tools powered by AI are damaging an essential part of what it means to be a developer and an engineer: **the learning process**. As humans, we learn from our mistakes. We learn from doing. When we are forced to make our brain work itself, we grow the most.

Throwing a question or error we aren't imemdiately familiar with into ChatGPT and taking its response like it's prophecy isn't healthy, and it sure as hell isn't going to turn you into the 10x engineer of your dreams.

It's especially depressing seeing how much AI has taken over students of all ages. There are too many college students that simply cannot write code for themselves. And I'm not talking about Leetcode—there are so many students nearing graduation (or have already graduated) that don't know the basics of Git, how to read an error message, or how to read documentation.

> The best part of programming is the satisfaction you get from builing things yourself. When you can proudly take ownership for something and say you brought an idea into life.

If you're a developer, especially one that is young or is actively trying to learn something new, then ditch the unnecessary AI tools. You don't need ChatGPT, Claude, or Cursor—do yourself a favor.
