# avoiding ai brainrot as a developer

there's a pandemic going around, and it isn't covid. it's artificial intelligence.

with ai being shoved into our faces daily, it's easy to become compliant to all of it. as much as i enjoy working with ai sometimes, i also don't want my skills as a developer to stagnate.

so here's how i'm avoiding brainrot as a developer, and how i actually use ai in my work.

### doing the work

i try to avoid having ai agents write the majority of my code. it's true they may save you a ton of time, but as someone that still has a lot to learn, i'd rather do things myself and gain the experience.

this also means i've disabled ai edit suggestions and/or autocompletions on most of my projects. *tab tab tab* is really freaking satisfying, but part of the fun of programming is actually writing code.

### flexing my brain muscles

whether it's leetcode or competitive programming, i'm doing my best to maintain what i already know and actively learn more.

there's a benefit to struggling. it's part of the problem solving process. it can be frustrating, but that's how things stick with me.

and when i'm truly stuck, i'm not reaching for ai immediately. i'm reading documentation and books. i'm experimenting. i'm debugging.

i'm a big believer that being handed the answers all the time isn't healthy.

### using ai effectively

i'm a big fan of using ai as a planning and review tool. that means i use it before and after the actually programming. it's made me a better architect and it's made me think about things i wouldn't have thought of by myself.

another application is pull request reviews. i like using ai to review my code before my real people do. my team's time is valuable, so ai catches minor bugs and nitpicks that allow them to focus on the real changes.
